Contributions

  1. Books- Frederick Brooks is the author of several books about computer science. A few of the most famous books are: The Design of Design: Essays from a Computer Scientist, and The Mythical Man-Month: Essays on Software Engineering, 20th Anniversary Edition.
  2. Research- He also conducts research, mainly in The Process of Design, Human-Computer Interaction, and 3D Interactive Computer Graphics.
  3. Work- He joined IBM working in New York, he was an architect of the Stretch and Harvest computers. There, he invented the term, “computer architecture”. He became manager of this computer team and led it to further development. His greatest technological achievement was changing the IBM 360 series from a 6-bit byte to an 8-bit byte, enabling the use of lowercase letters.
  4. 2005 January 2005, he gave the annual Turing lecture to the IEE/BCS on Collaboration and Telecollaboration in Design.
